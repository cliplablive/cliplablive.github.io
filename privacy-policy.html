ClipLab Live Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 4, 2026

Overview

ClipLab Live is a creator publishing platform for preparing and publishing short-form livestream highlight videos. This Privacy Policy explains how ClipLab Live collects, uses, stores, shares, and deletes information when creators connect publishing accounts and use the platform.

Information We Collect

  • Account authorization data from connected platforms, including OAuth access tokens and refresh tokens when provided by the platform.
  • Basic account information returned by connected platforms, such as account identifier, display name, profile image, or username, when needed to confirm the authorized account.
  • Generated video files, captions, titles, hashtags, source credit, thumbnails or cover candidates, and publishing status metadata.
  • Operational logs needed to diagnose upload status, API errors, retries, and cleanup.
  • Contact information voluntarily provided by a user, such as an email address used for support or removal requests.

How We Use Information

  • To authenticate a creator through OAuth and connect the requested publishing integration.
  • To upload or publish approved short-form videos to the creator's connected account.
  • To display account connection status, upload status, retry status, and publishing history.
  • To generate or store captions, hashtags, source credit, and other metadata selected for publishing.
  • To detect failures, prevent duplicate uploads, troubleshoot integrations, and delete local files after successful publishing when cleanup is enabled.

TikTok Data

When TikTok is connected, ClipLab Live uses TikTok OAuth and the TikTok Content Posting API only for the scopes approved by the user and TikTok. The integration is used to identify the connected TikTok account and upload or publish videos selected through the ClipLab Live workflow. ClipLab Live does not request TikTok passwords.

Sharing

ClipLab Live does not sell personal information. Information is shared only as needed to operate the service, such as sending an approved video, caption, and metadata to a connected platform through that platform's API, or when required by law or a valid rights/removal request.

Storage, Retention, And Deletion

Generated video files may be stored temporarily while awaiting upload, retry, or deletion. OAuth tokens are retained only while the integration remains connected. Publishing logs and status records may be retained for troubleshooting and audit history. Users may request disconnection, token deletion, or content removal by contacting support.

Security

ClipLab Live uses reasonable technical measures to protect tokens and publishing data, including limiting access to authorized operators and avoiding storage of social account passwords. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

User Choices

  • You may disconnect a platform integration by revoking OAuth access in the connected platform or by contacting ClipLab Live support.
  • You may request deletion of stored OAuth tokens, local generated videos, and publishing metadata associated with your account.
  • You may request correction or removal of content that you believe was published in error.

Children

ClipLab Live is not directed to children under 13 and should not be used by anyone who is not permitted to use the connected publishing platforms.

Contact
